    David Brenner was an American stand-up comedian, actor, television personality, radio personality, and author. Many believe that he “was a pioneer of observational comedy”. He frequently appeared as a guest on The Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson in the 1970s and 1980s. During the course of his career, David Brenner was also a part of several other projects such as Modern Family, Arli$$, Snip, The Real Tom Kennedy Show, The Ed Sullivan Show, The Virginia Graham Show, The David Frost Show, When Jews Were Funny, The Dean Martin Comedy World, The Bobby Vinton Show, American Bandstand, The Mike Douglas Show, The Merv Griffin Show, Real Time With Bill Maher, Late Night with Conan O’Brien, The Daily Show, The Tragic Side of Comedy, The Tonight Show with Jay Leno, Just for Laughs, and Late Show with David Letterman.

    David Brenner Facts

  • He spent his childhood years growing up in South and West Philadelphia.
  • David’s father gave up his career and a film contract in order to please Brenner’s grandfather, a rabbi, as the latter objected to his working on the Sabbath.
  • David Brenner went on to spend 2 years in the U.S. Army after completing high school and served in the 101st Airborne and as a cryptographer of the 595th Signal Corps in Böblingen, Germany.
  • He was cast as a Charity Ball Auctioneer in the 1989 romantic comedy film Worth Winning, starring Mark Harmon, Madeleine Stowe, and Lesley Ann Warren.
  • One of his friends was actor, comedian, and writer Richard Lewis who described David Brenner as “the king of hip, observational comedy.”
