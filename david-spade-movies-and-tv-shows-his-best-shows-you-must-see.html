    David Spade is an actor best known for his offbeat humor. Although he started acting in the late 1980s, he rose to fame by being among the cast members of the American late-night live television series. Saturday Night Live , 1990’s. He has had a somewhat successful career in the film industry having appeared in more than seventy films and TV shows.

    Following his on-screen appearances, Spade received numerous awards and nominations, including the MTV Movie Award for Best On-Screen Duo in Tommy Boy and the Razzie Award for Worst Supporting Actress in Jack & Jill . The veteran actor was also awarded a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame for his contributions to the film industry as a writer, actor and producer. If you are a fan of this amazing actor, below are the details of his movies and TV shows as well as his best must see shows.

    David Spades Movies and TV Shows

    The award-winning actor-turned-screenwriter landed his first film role as Kyle in the 1987 comedy film. Police Academy 4: Citizens on Patrol . In 1992, David Spade was cast as Theological Cokehead in Light Sleepers and went on to appear in the comic book sci-fi, Coneheads the following year. Three years later he starred in the American road comedy film, Tommy Boy as Richard Hayden. Since then he has acted in numerous films including A Very Brady Sequel (1996), Pointless (1998), The Benchwarmers (2006), Growing Up (2010), Father of the Year(2018).

    Spade made his television debut in the 1998 television series, The Facts of Life as Scott and went on to appear in the American action drama series, Baywatch the following year. Other series he has acted in are Monster (1990), Beavis and Butthead (1994), Just Shoot Me! (1997-2003), 8 Simple Rules (2004-2005), Rules of Engagement (2007-2013), Hot in Cleveland (2012), Roadies (2016), The Mayor (2017) among others. He also lent his voice to The Rugrats Movie (1998),The Emperor’s New Groove (2000), Hotel Transylvania Franchise (2012-2018) and others.

    His writing credits include Saturday Night Live (1990-1996) Found Lost (1999), Joe Dirt (2001), Dickie Roberts; Former child star (2003), Crazy Families (2017) among others. Additionally, the trained writer has been credited with producing Joe Dirt 2: Handsome Loser and The Showbiz Show with David Spade (2005-2007). Keep scrolling for a rundown of the best David Spade shows you must see.

    His best must-see shows

    5. Saturday Night Live (1990-1996)

    Popular on NBC’s late-night live television, the variety show that David Spade starred in along with other comedians in the 1990s ranks among his finest television show performances. Spade played various roles as a cast member on the variety show, which is hosted by various celebrity guests, who usually deliver the opening monologue and perform comedy sketches with the various cast members. The TV show earned him a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Writing for a Comedy Series.

    4. Just shoot me! (1997 – 2003)

    This NBC comedy television sitcom stars David Spade as excellently portrayed Dennis Finch, the secretary of a fashion magazine, Blush. He starred alongside Laura San Giacomo (Maya), a spirited journalist who has fired again and has been unable to find a job elsewhere. She is then forced to work for her father’s fashion magazine, Blush. This absolute joy in television earned David Spade nominations at the Golden Globe Awards, the Primetime Emmy Awards and the American Comedy Awards.

    3. 8 simple rules (2004 – 2005)

    While it doesn’t necessarily have to be the best TV show David Spade has been in, it’s certainly one of his most fascinating TV shows. He portrays CJ Barnes, who was once in the Army and claims to have studied aerial photography during the Gulf War and received a Purple Heart in combat, in complete contrast to his generally childish attitude. The comedy-drama TV show won 9 awards and 12 nominations.

    4. The Showbiz Show with David Spade (2005 – 2007)

    This comedy tv show is a comedic parody of celebrity news shows. Entertainment Weekly and Access Hollywood starred David Spade as a comedian on Comedy Central. The weekly television show was developed from Spade’s Hollywood minute segment on Saturday Night Live and it is without a doubt one of the most captivating TV shows you will ever watch.

    1. Rules of Engagement (2007 – 2013)

    Of the many characters that David Spade has, it can be boldly said that this is one of the most entertaining. He plays the role of Russell Dunbar, a passionate manager of his father’s real estate and development business, Dunbar Industries, with Patrick Warburton, Megyn Price, Oliver Hudson and others. With an award-winning performance, David Spade went home with a Teen Choice Award nomination for Choice TV Actor: Comedy.
