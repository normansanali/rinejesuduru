    Glenda Hatchett Biography

    Glenda Hatchett is an accomplished American attorney and TV personality born and raised in  Atlanta, Georgia. Currently, she is the star of the reality court show ‘The Verdict with Judge Hatchett’ and the former reality star of the former court show ‘Judge Hatchett’. She is also the founding partner at the national law firm, The Hatchett Firm.

    Hatchett is also a motivational speaker and is mostly invited to events and conferences as a keynote speaker. In 2013 during the Pennsylvania Conference for Women 10th Anniversary, she was the opening keynote speaker. She is the only one in the Conference’s 10-year history to be invited to speak more than once.

    Hatchett has featured as a guest commentator and legal analyst on national issues on many media outlets such as CNN, The View, Jimmy KimmelLive, The Mo’Nique Show, and The Young and the Restless.

    Glenda Delta Air Lines

    Hatchet completed a federal clerkship in the United States District Court in the Northern District of Georgia. She later joined Delta Air Lines serving both as senior attorney and public relations manager becoming the airline’s highest-ranking woman of color worldwide.

    As a senior attorney, Hatchett litigated cases in federal courts throughout the country, and as Manager of Public Relations, she supervised global crisis management, and media relations for all of Europe, Asia, and the United States.

    In January 1990 Hatchett was named “100 Best and Brightest Black Women in Corporate America” by Ebony Magazine. She was awarded the Emory Medal, the highest award given to an alum by the university, due to her commitment to excellence and service within the community.

    Glenda Hatchett Delta Fulton County

    In 1990 Hatchett was appointed as Chief Presiding Judge of the Fulton County, Georgia Juvenile Court becoming Georgia’s first African-American Chief Presiding Judge of a state court and the department head of one of the largest juvenile court systems in the country.

    Judge Glenda Hatchett

    In 2000 she quit Fulton County and started presiding over Judge Hatchett, a nationally syndicated TV Show. The show was nominated for Emmy awards in 2008 and 2009 for the Outstanding Legal/Courtroom Program. The show ran for 13 seasons before it ended.

    Hatchet has worked as a board of directors for Gap, Inc. the Hospital Corporation of America (HCA), and The Service Master Company. Since 2004 she has been a board member of the Atlanta Falcons Football Organization and serves on the Board of Advisors for Play Pumps International. She also serves on the National Board of Governors for the Boys and Girls Clubs of America National Board of Governors.

    Glenda Hatchett Law Firm

    The Hatchett Firm, PC

    In 2014 she co-founded The Hatchett Firm, PC in Atlanta. The firm deals with Catastrophic Personal Injury, Medical Malpractice, and Class Action lawsuits. In addition to practicing law.
