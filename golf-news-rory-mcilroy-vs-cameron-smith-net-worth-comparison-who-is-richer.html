    Rory McIlroy and Cameron Smith are the two most prominent names on the rival tours PGA and LIV golf, respectively. After winning the CJ cup this year, McIlroy reached on top of the best golfer list. Last, he was seen playing at the DP World Tour Championship at Jumeirah Golf Estates – Earth Course. 

    The Ireland golfer is against the Saudi-backed tour, whereas Smith loves being part of it. He is finally back in his homeland after three years. And it was possible because the first LIV event of 2023 will take place in Australia. Currently, he is playing at the Australian PGA Championship. Both golfers have done fantastic in their golf careers, but who values more when it comes to money? Let’s find out.

    The net worth of Rory McIlroy in 2022

    Rory McIlroy is a professional golfer, a member of the PGA, and on European tours. He has had splendid victories and registered four Major titles under his name. The Ireland golfer was one of the four players who won the first four majors of their life by the age of 25. It also includes Jack Nicklaus and Tiger Woods. According to reports, his estimated net worth must be around $220 million.

    In terms of earnings, the current number-one golfer is only one step behind the former number-one golfer, Woods. In 2020 alone, he earned $50 million. Also, they estimated his PGA earnings to be more than $80 million, while on the European tour, they estimated it to be more than $35 million.

    Many people might not know, but European golfers earn a hefty sum for only participating in the tournament. They are paid huge amounts of money to be part of events like the Abu Dhabi Championship and the Dubai Desert Classic. However, according to reports, the pro golfer turned down a $2.5 million offer to attend the event two years ago.

    The net worth of Cameron Smith in 2022

    The Australian professional golfer is now part of LIV golf. According to Celebrity Net Worth, the net worth of the golfer is estimated to be around $50 million. Before moving on to LIV, Smith was part of the PGA tour, where he achieved great heights. He won the Open Championship in 2022, followed by the Players Championship this year. However, he finished in second place at the 2020 Masters tournament.

    Smith lives in a lavish $2.4 million mansion, situated in Jacksonville, Florida. During the middle of August this year, the Aussie golfer moved to the Saudi-backed circuit LIV. He joined the tour and signed a contract of $100 million. They considered his payday to be one of the enormous paydays earned by a player that left the PGA and moved to LIV.
