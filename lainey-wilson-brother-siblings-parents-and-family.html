    Lainey Wilson’s Musical Style

    While Wilson’s music is firmly grounded in the country genre, it also incorporates pop, contemporary country, southern rock, and classic country elements.

    Mark Deming of AllMusic praised Wilson’s distinctively Southern voice and described her songs as a bold and emotional blend of contemporary country, vintage Southern rock, classic rock, and modern pop.

    Wilson referred to her musical style as “bell-bottom country,” a unique blend of soothing melodies and unflinching honesty described by Taste of Country.

    Dolly Parton has significantly impacted Wilson’s music, as evidenced by her tribute to the country legend in the form of a self-written track titled “WWDD” (What Would Dolly Do).

    Lainey Wilson acknowledges Lee Ann Womack as a significant influence on her music and her journey as an artist.
