    Wale | Career

    Wale’s music career began in 2006 when he got a deal with Studio 43, a local label.

    In the same year, he released the track “Dig Dug,” which became the most requested song by any local artist in Washington, DC.

    His enormous popularity earned him the WKY’s Go-Go Awards, DC Metro Breakthrough Artist of the Year.

    “100 Miles & Running,” his third mixtape, was released in 2007. Similarly, in 2011, “Ambition,” his second album, was released.

    It was preceded by a promotional mixtape called “The Eleven One Eleven Theory,” which went viral and helped boost album sales.

    Likewise, “It’s Complicated,” “Self-Promotion,” and “Free Lunch” were Wale’s three EPs released in 2018.

    Wale’s sixth studio album, “Wow… That’s Crazy,” was released in 2019.

    In addition, in 2021, Wale released his seventh studio album, “Folarin II,” following up on his 2012 mixtape Folarin.
